  Blind Detection and Acoustic Inpainting for Telephony Speech Restoration}\\[2pt]
{\small MIT MBAn GenAI Lab\quad|\quad May 2026\quad|\quad v5.0}

\vspace{2pt}
\noindent\textbf{Abstract.}\;{\small
We present an end-to-end pipeline for restoring telephony speech corrupted by
packet-loss gaps without prior knowledge of cut locations.
Using the Harper Valley banking corpus~\cite{harpervalley}, SHA256-seeded
cuts placed exclusively in VAD-detected speech regions simulate packet loss
under two fill modes: hard zeros and RFC\,3389 comfort-noise.
Four complementary blind detectors recover cut timestamps: RMS energy
dropout (A), adaptive MFCC discontinuity (B), WavLM frame-embedding
cosine distance~\cite{wavlm} (C), and Silero VAD~\cite{silero} (D).
A pre-detection step caches all four methods' output once;
nine experiment configurations then sweep detector subsets and
intersection strategies without re-running detection.
Gaps are repaired via Qwen3-ASR~\cite{qwen3}\,/\,Whisper~\cite{whisper},
Qwen3-32B text correction, and PlayDiffusion~\cite{playdiffusion}.
Correct-action rate (CAR) and word-error rate (WER) measure downstream
agent impact across three conditions: clean\,(ceiling),
distorted\,(floor), and restored\,(system under test).
Evaluation on 40 Harper Valley utterances (8 tasks $\times$ 5 files)
reveals that \emph{all nine} inpainting configurations increase WER
relative to the distorted floor (best: $+0.033$; worst: $+0.120$).
Strikingly, the ground-truth-oracle run (\textit{no\_detector},
WER\,=\,0.213) is the single worst configuration, demonstrating that
inpainting quality---not detection precision---is the primary
bottleneck.
CAR remains flat at 80.0\,\% for eight of nine restored conditions,
equal to the distorted floor, exposing keyword-classifier insensitivity
as a confound.
The pipeline is net-negative for transcript fidelity in all current
configurations.
}

\section{Dataset and Distortion Model}
\label{sec:distortion}

The \textbf{Gridspace Stanford Harper Valley} corpus~\cite{harpervalley}
provides real telephony conversations for 8 banking actions at 16\,kHz mono.
\texttt{generate\_distorted\_audio.py} first runs an energy-based VAD on 20\,ms
frames, labelling speech whenever RMS exceeds
$\tau{=}\hat\mu_\mathrm{ns}+0.02(\hat\sigma_\mathrm{pk}{-}\hat\mu_\mathrm{ns})$.
Four cuts per file, drawn uniformly from $[1,200]$\,ms, are placed
\emph{exclusively inside speech regions}; distortion is seeded by
SHA256(\textit{stem})[:8] for full determinism.
A second fill mode (\texttt{--fill-mode\,comfort-noise}) replaces zeroed
samples with bandpass-filtered Gaussian noise ($300$--$3\,400$\,Hz) scaled to
the local noise floor and cosine-faded at boundaries---modelling RFC\,3389
comfort-noise injection.

\section{Cut Taxonomy}
\label{sec:taxonomy}

Packet-loss cuts vary along two dimensions---\emph{where} the gap falls
and \emph{how long} it lasts---each requiring a different handling
strategy.  Figure~2 illustrates the three location types;
Table~1 summarises routing.

\noindent\textbf{Location.}
\emph{(i) Inter-word silence:} gap within a natural pause; crossfade
suffices; pre-context check may suppress the event.
\emph{(ii) Word boundary:} gap straddles two words; both tokens are
outside the gap, so crossfade is applied.
\emph{(iii) Mid-word:} gap within one token; for ${<}20$\,ms crossfade
is transparent; for longer gaps adaptive MFCC selects TTS or PlayDiffusion.

\noindent\textbf{Duration.}
Tier\,A (20--200\,ms) causes word-level loss; Tier\,B (5--20\,ms) a
click; Tier\,C/D ($<$5\,ms) sub-threshold.  Comfort-noise (Tier\,E)
defeats all signal-processing detectors; neural methods~(C, D) are
required.

\section{Blind Cut Detection (v5.0)}
\label{sec:detection}

Version 5.0 adds two neural detectors (C\,\&\,D) alongside the two
signal-processing methods from prior versions.

\noindent\textbf{A — RMS Energy Dropout.}
Contiguous samples with $|x[n]|\leq10^{-4}$ for $\geq0.5$\,ms are
flagged.  A 5\,ms pre-context check discards regions where the preceding
audio was already quiet (natural pauses).

\noindent\textbf{B — MFCC Discontinuity (adaptive).}
13-dimensional MFCC frame distances are computed at 10\,ms hop.
The detection threshold is $\theta = \mathrm{median}(d) +
k\cdot\mathrm{MAD}(d)$ floored at the 99th percentile, where
$k{=}3$.  This adapts to per-file noise conditions and avoids the
fixed constant of prior versions.

\noindent\textbf{C — WavLM Frame-Embedding Discontinuity.}
\texttt{microsoft/wavlm-base-plus}~\cite{wavlm} (95\,M params) produces
768-dim embeddings at 50\,Hz via CNN downsampling of 16\,kHz audio.
Cosine distance between adjacent frames flags embedding discontinuities
that correspond to phonetic content loss invisible to spectral methods.

\noindent\textbf{D — Silero VAD.}
The 8\,MB Silero VAD model~\cite{silero} classifies 30\,ms frames as
speech or non-speech.  Gaps between adjacent speech segments
(${>}$20\,ms minimum) within an otherwise active utterance are treated
as dropout candidates---directly modelling packet silence insertion.
Unlike RMS, Silero is robust to comfort-noise fills.

\noindent\textbf{Fusion.}
\texttt{merge\_events\_with\_source()} pools events from all four
detectors, tagging each with its source(s).  Nine
\texttt{events\_source} modes are evaluated (Table~2): four single-method,
three intersection pairs, one union, and the ground-truth oracle.

\noindent\textbf{Benchmark.}
On 125 synthetic telephony clips (5 tiers A--E), the pre-v5.0 silence
and spectral-flux detectors already achieve P\,=\,R\,=\,1.00 on
zero-fill Tiers A--D.  Methods C\,\&\,D are evaluated on the 40 Harper
Valley ground-truth files via the standalone benchmark cell (Step\,7a)
before any inpainting.

\section{Audio Inpainting}
\label{sec:inpainting}

Each gap passes through three chained models on Google Colab (T4 GPU).

\noindent\textbf{Transcription.}
\textbf{Qwen3-ASR-1.7B}~\cite{qwen3} generates word-level timestamps;
\textbf{Whisper}~\cite{whisper} provides a fallback.

\noindent\textbf{Correction.}
The broken transcript is sent to \textbf{Qwen3-32B}~\cite{qwen3}
(OpenRouter) to infer the intended sentence near the cut.

\noindent\textbf{PlayDiffusion}~\cite{playdiffusion} fills gaps under
three routing rules (Table~1):
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item \emph{Short or silence gap:} ${<}20$\,ms or no ASR word overlap;
  linear crossfade only.
\item \emph{Acoustic glitch:} adaptive MFCC $L_2$ above the per-file
  95th-percentile baseline; overlapping tokens removed to force
  full regeneration.
\item \emph{Normal mid-word gap:} \texttt{InpaintInput} with ASR-snapped
  word boundaries and LLM-corrected \texttt{output\_text}.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent\textbf{Pre-Detection Cache.}
PlayDiffusion is loaded once per session.  Before the experiment loop,
a dedicated cell runs all four detectors on every file and saves
\texttt{output\_dir/detected/B\#\#\_\#\#.json} (all modes).  The loop
reads from this cache: detection cost is $O(N)$ rather than $O(9N)$.

\section{Discussion and Limitations}
\label{sec:discussion}

\noindent\textbf{Central finding: inpainting degrades WER in all
configurations.}
Every restored condition exhibits higher WER than the distorted floor
(Table\,4).
The most damning evidence is \textit{no\_detector}, which uses
ground-truth gap timestamps and therefore bypasses detection entirely.
Its WER of 0.213 is the worst among all restored conditions---more
than twice the distorted baseline of 0.101.
This establishes that the bottleneck is not detection quality but
\emph{inpainting quality}: PlayDiffusion + Qwen3 together introduce
more ASR errors than the original gaps did.

\noindent\textbf{Over-detection in signal-processing methods.}
Method\,A (RMS) proposes 617 events for 160 ground-truth gaps---a
$3.86\times$ over-detection rate.
The union \textit{auto\_any} inherits this pathology (669 events; RMS
effectively dominates the union).
Each false-positive event routes correct audio through PlayDiffusion,
synthesizing speech that Whisper misreads.
The result is net acoustic degradation even when the gap-filling itself
is locally reasonable.
Method\,B (MFCC) is the only signal-processing detector with an
acceptable F$_1$ (0.508), yet it still generates 1.21$\times$ more
events than GT.
The intersection \textit{auto\_rms\_mfcc} (A$\cap$B) prunes false
positives to F$_1$\,=\,0.456 at the cost of halving recall (0.438).

\noindent\textbf{WavLM intersection: ``do less, hurt less''.}
The three lowest-WER configurations are
\textit{auto\_wavlm\_silero} (0.134),
\textit{auto\_wavlm\_rms} (0.142), and
\textit{auto\_wavlm} alone (0.145).
This is not evidence that WavLM-guided inpainting is effective.
\textit{auto\_wavlm} fires on only 18 of 160 gaps (recall\,=\,0.037);
its intersections fire on 11--15.
These configurations achieve low WER precisely \emph{because they leave
most of the audio untouched}---they apply PlayDiffusion to fewer than
10\,\% of gaps, limiting their opportunity to introduce new errors.
A configuration that fires on zero events would achieve WER identical
to the distorted baseline ($0.101$).
The WER ranking therefore measures harm-avoidance, not gap-repair.

\noindent\textbf{MFCC hallucination: a concrete failure mode.}
\textit{auto\_mfcc} uniquely drops two additional utterances
(B03\_04, B03\_05) from the \texttt{check\_balance} task,
reducing its CAR to 75.0\,\% vs.\ 80.0\,\% for all other configurations.
In both cases the clean Whisper transcript contains the phrase
``\textit{check my account balance},'' which triggers the keyword hit.
Post-inpainting, Whisper reads
``\textit{check my account valorant}'' (B03\_04) and
``\textit{check my account down list}'' (B03\_05)---phonetically plausible
but semantically incorrect substitutions generated by PlayDiffusion
when MFCC falsely flags the \emph{balance} word region as a gap.
This illustrates how false-positive detection directly produces task
errors through the inpainting chain.

\noindent\textbf{B06\_04: fragile keyword boundary.}
File B06\_04 (\texttt{reset\_password}) is the only utterance in the
dataset that the distorted condition gets wrong but clean does not.
The speaker's phrase ``\textit{I would like to reset my password}''
is rendered by Whisper as ``\textit{semi-password}'' on the clean
audio---barely triggering the \texttt{password} keyword.
After distortion (and all restored conditions), Whisper renders
``\textit{semi-pastured}'' or ``\textit{semi-passured}''---neither
matching the keyword.
The distortion removes a phoneme boundary that Whisper needed to
recover the keyword.
No restored configuration recovers this file: the inpainted waveform
produces the same ``\textit{pastured}'' hallucination, indicating
that the synthesized fill does not reconstruct the critical phoneme.

\noindent\textbf{Systematic \texttt{pay\_bill} failure.}
All 5 \texttt{pay\_bill} utterances fail under every condition,
including clean (Table\,6, highlighted row).
The callers use locutions such as
``\textit{I need to pay my utility bill}'' and
``\textit{can I set up automatic payments?}'',
none of which contains the exact substring
``\textit{pay bill}'', ``\textit{auto pay}'', or ``\textit{bill payment}''
after Whisper transcription.
This is a classifier design failure, not a restoration failure: the
keyword list does not cover caller paraphrase variation.
These 5 errors are present in the clean ceiling and therefore do not
affect the relative comparison between distorted and restored, but they
inflate the apparent gap between ceiling (82.5\,\%) and floor (80.0\,\%).
Excluding \texttt{pay\_bill}, clean achieves 94.3\,\% CAR vs.\
91.4\,\% for distorted---a 2.9\,pp degradation more representative
of the true cost of telephony distortion.

\noindent\textbf{CAR metric insensitivity.}
Across the 9 restored configurations, CAR varies from 75.0\,\% to
80.0\,\%---a 5\,pp range versus a 0.087-unit WER range
(0.134--0.221).
The keyword classifier absorbs substantial ASR noise: as long as any
keyword from the correct task appears \emph{somewhere} in the
transcript, the classification succeeds.
Inpainting errors that corrupt auxiliary words (amounts, names, phone
numbers) are invisible to CAR.
WER exposes this damage directly.
A stronger downstream evaluator---e.g., a prompted LLM classifier or
a fine-tuned intent model---would likely widen the CAR spread and
provide a more informative signal.

\noindent\textbf{Implications.}
The hypothesis that speech restoration measurably improves agent
correct-action rate is \emph{not confirmed} on this dataset.
The current pipeline is net-negative for transcript quality (WER)
and does not improve CAR over the distorted baseline for any
configuration.
Two sub-problems must be solved independently before a second
evaluation:
(1) \emph{detection precision}: reduce false-positive rates,
particularly for RMS, to near zero before inpainting is applied;
(2) \emph{inpainting fidelity}: the GT-oracle experiment
(\textit{no\_detector}, WER\,=\,0.213) shows that PlayDiffusion +
Qwen3 cannot yet faithfully reconstruct the missing speech---
the inpainted region must be phonemically accurate enough that
Whisper can recover the intended keyword.
Until both conditions are met, telephony distortion is less
harmful than the restoration attempt.
